Building My First Power BI Dashboard

Mahwish Farhan | Jun 21, 2026 min read

Creating my first Power BI dashboard was an exciting milestone in my data analytics journey. It helped me turn raw data into meaningful insights and taught me how powerful visualization can be in storytelling. Building this dashboard was an eye‑opening experience into how global economic shifts impact companies, industries, and entire regions.

1. Project Overview

I built a World Layoffs - Insignts & Trends dashboard to analyze worldwide layoffs from 2020 to 2024, covering more than 1,000 companies, 31 industries, and nearly 485,000 employees affected. The goal of this project was to understand how layoffs evolved globally during and after the pandemic. I wanted to explore:

  • Which companies had the highest layoffs
  • Which industries were most affected
  • How layoffs changed year‑over‑year
  • Which regions experienced the largest impact
  • Monthly patterns and deep‑dive trends

Tool Used: Power BI Desktop
Dataset: Global Layoffs (2020–2024) — includes company details, industry, country, layoff counts, percentage laid off, funds raised, and timeline fields

2. Data Preparation

Before creating visuals, I performed data cleaning and transformation using Power Query Editor, ensuring accuracy and consistancy before modeling.

Key Steps Included:

  • Removing duplicates and null values
  • Standardizing industry and country names
  • Converting date fields into proper formats
  • Creating Year, Month, and Quarter columns
  • Adding calculated fields for YoY change and total layoffs
  • Ensuring numeric fields (layoff count, funds raised) were properly typed

3. Dashboard Design

I designed a two‑page dashboard to separate high‑level insights from deeper analysis.

Page 1: Global Layoff Overview

Focused on global patterns and top‑level metrics.

Key visuals:

  • KPI Cards: Total layoffs, average layoff %, max single-company layoff
  • Top 10 Companies by Layoffs
  • Industry‑Wise Layoffs (Pie Chart)
  • Yearly Totals + YoY Change
  • Global Map (Layoffs by Country)

Page 2: Detailed Layoff Insights & Deep Dive

This page explores detailed patterns and company‑level insights.

Key visuals:

  • Companies with 100% Layoffs (shutdowns)
  • Layoffs by Country & Industry (stacked bar)
  • Monthly Layoffs Trend (2020–2024)

My goal was a clean, intuitive layout that allows users to move from a global view to detailed insights seamlessly.

4. Key Insights

Tech and Retail were the hardest hit

Tech companies like Amazon, Tesla, Dell, Google, Meta, and SAP dominated the Top 10 list. Retail and Transportation also showed significant layoffs due to shifting consumer behavior and supply chain disruptions.

Layoffs peaked in 2023

The YoY chart revealed a sharp spike in 2023 — the highest in the dataset. 2024 showed early signs of stabilization.

Several companies shut down completely

The “100% Layoffs” table highlighted companies that ceased operations, including:

  • Katerra (Construction)
  • Bitwise (Tech)
  • Zulily (Retail)
  • Convoy (Logistics)

These cases often involved startups with high fundraising but unsustainable cost structures.

North America and Europe saw the largest impact

The map visualization showed the highest concentration of layoffs in the US, Canada, UK, and Germany.

Monthly trends showed seasonal patterns

Layoffs often spiked in Q1, aligning with annual restructuring cycles.

5. Challenges Faced

Like any real‑world project, this one came with its own learning curve:

  • Understanding how to model multiple dimensions (Company, Industry, Country, Date)
  • Choosing visuals that clearly communicate trends
  • Designing a layout that works across two pages
  • Syncing slicers across pages for a smooth user experience
  • Ensuring the dashboard remained clean despite large amounts of data

Each challenge helped me improve my BI design and analytical thinking.

6. What I Learned

This project strengthened my skills in:

  • Data cleaning and transformation using Power Query
  • Data modeling with a star schema
  • DAX calculations for YoY metrics and KPIs
  • Dashboard storytelling — turning raw data into meaningful insights
  • Designing multi‑page Power BI reports
  • Creating interactive visuals that support deeper exploration

Most importantly, I learned how to structure a dashboard that feels professional, intuitive, and portfolio‑ready.

Final Thoughts

The World Layoffs dashboard helped me understand how global events ripple across industries and regions. It also allowed me to demonstrate my ability to work with complex datasets, build insightful visuals, and communicate findings clearly.

This project reflects my passion for data storytelling and my commitment to building dashboards that deliver real value.
